He presented himself at her bedroom window and entered. Much to his surprise, there were several young girls in the bedroom with Klaas. It was said that he called out, "Which one of you is Polly Klaas?" Either way, Davis went to Klaas and held a knife to her throat. He exited with her through the bedroom window he had entered, leaving her friends behind who were, at that point, eyewitnesses to the abduction of Polly Klaas.

Davis took Klaas to his car. They drove away from Polly's house in Petaluma. Instead of going to a certain location with Polly, Davis instead drove around with her in his car for some time that night. On at least one occasion, Davis stopped at a gas station allowing Polly to enter and use the restroom. He said later that he was surprised to see her return to his car as he had thought she would have surely made a run for it. Something very dramatic must have occurred between the time she returned to his car and the time she was ultimately murdered by Davis. According to the general consensus, Davis killed Klaas to keep her quiet after raping her. But could there have been another reason?

Davis was later arrested and further identified using composite drawings that were based on the descriptions of other young girls who were in Polly Klaas's bedroom on the night of her abduction. Davis admitted to the abduction and ultimate murder of Polly Klaas. He even led investigators to her remains. He was the model cooperative suspect in a murder case - almost too perfect. Davis was convicted for the kidnapping and murder of Polly Klaas. He is currently on death row at San Quentin Prison outside San Francisco.
